Baltimore, Maryland

History of Baltimore

Baltimore was founded in 1797 and has a rich history and place in the United States. The city first served as one of the first major American ports along with Boston, MS and grew in size and importance. During the civil war Baltimore's Fort McHenry became the birthplace of the American national anthem, written by Francis Scott Key during the War of 1812. Baltimore is also the home of Frederick Douglass, American Speechwriter and embassador to the President in the 19th century.

Things to Do

Baltimore has a lot of fun and interesting neighborhoods and activities to engage in. Fells Point is a histoic and hip neighborhood home to the H&M bakery. The inner harbor features lots of interesting shops and a view of Federal Hill. The Baltimore Musuem of Art is a historic art museum near John's Hopkins University.
